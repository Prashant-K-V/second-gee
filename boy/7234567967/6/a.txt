Studies suggest that middle child may not be very family oriented but they come up with some special traits in the long run. They are believed to be more successful, creative, fair-minded and independent than their siblings. Katrin Schumann, co-author of The Secret Power of Middle Children tells Psychology Today opines, “Although middles are neglected, research concludes they actually benefit from this in the long run. They become more independent, think out of the box, feel less pressure to conform, and are more empathetic.”
